Questions

This is just a random thought that popped into my brain. Are we allowed to question in India?

Home

I have never thought that a simple question like “Does God exist?” can land you in trouble at home. This is a very deep and debate mongering question. But I think this fundamental question of existence can be easily dodged by your family members. Maybe God is like our politicians. He/She doesn’t like questions. Can I ask about the validity of inter-religion marriages at my home? Hell no! Starting at a very tender age and until your death, you will see your family members not being able to answer fundamental things.

Outside Your Home

This one needs no introduction. Indian conservative society doesn’t allow questions. That’s a fact. Ever questioned your relatives? If yes, then you must have been given a lecture about how to drift in the societal river. Your government doesn’t like being questioned (Don’t wanna start on that one!).

Questions are a fundamental requirement for the progress of society. A nation where questions are suppressed produces a citizenry of sheep. They just follow others. A simple question can lead to inventions. A simple question can lead to a change in entire political systems.

A simple question leads to independence.
